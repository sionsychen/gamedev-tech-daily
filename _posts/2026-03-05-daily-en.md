## Behind the Scenes: UE5 Surreal Puzzle Adventure 'Keeper'

<div class="article-meta">
  <span class="article-category">#UE5</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Lumen</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Technical Breakdown</span>
</div>

Double Fine Productions has released an in-depth technical breakdown of their latest title, "Keeper," a surreal atmospheric puzzle adventure centered around a forgotten lighthouse. The project, led by Director Lee Petty along with Animation Lead Zach Baharov, Senior Environment Artist Will Koehler, and Designer Asif Siddiky, showcases the studio's mastery of Unreal Engine 5's capabilities.

The development team leveraged UE5's Lumen global illumination system extensively to create the game's distinctive painterly lighting atmosphere. Character animation was implemented using Control Rigs, enabling fluid and expressive movements for the peculiar seabird protagonist. Notably, the environment art team employed VR sculpting techniques for world-building, allowing artists to intuitively shape the fantastical setting. Camera work represents another highlight, with each shot carefully designed to strengthen narrative impact. The game tells its whimsical and poignant story entirely without words, relying on visual storytelling and other non-verbal means of expression.

## Creating a Mystical Forest in Two Days: SpeedTree, Substance 3D & UE5 Workflow

<div class="article-meta">
  <span class="article-category">#Environment Art</span>
  <span class="article-category">#SpeedTree</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Substance3D</span>
</div>

Principal Production Artist Young Kwon Kim has shared a forest environment project completed in just two days, demonstrating how strategic tool selection and workflow optimization can yield high-quality results under tight deadlines.

The technical stack centered on Unreal Engine 5, complemented by SpeedTree for vegetation generation, Quixel Megascans for base scanned assets, and Substance 3D Designer and Painter for material authoring. The primary challenge was achieving an "ethereal and mystical" atmosphere within severe time constraints. The solution involved carefully calibrated volumetric fog and light shafts, combined with complex landscape materials and layered texture systems. Foliage distribution utilized PCG (Procedural Content Generation) workflows to ensure natural density variations and avoid the repetitive patterns typical of manual placement. Hero assets, including the central trees, were custom-crafted, while water animation employed the Fluid Flux plugin for enhanced realism. This case study offers valuable insights for teams needing rapid environment prototyping.

## Unity Asset Store to Delist Greater China Publisher Assets: Over 3,000 Resources Affected

<div class="article-meta">
  <span class="article-category">#Industry News</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Unity</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Policy Change</span>
</div>

Unity Technologies announced this week via email to developers that assets from Greater China publishers (mainland China, Hong Kong, and Macau) will be delisted from the Unity Asset Store effective March 31, 2026. The decision stems from "updated regional licensing, distribution, and compliance policy requirements." According to the published list, approximately 3,000 assets will be affected.

For existing purchasers, assets will remain functional in current projects but will no longer receive updates or technical support. More significantly, organizations located in Greater China will lose access to the global Unity Asset Store entirely after March 31, including all previously purchased assets. Unity has added a "Download All" feature to facilitate backups before the deadline. Refunds are available for assets purchased within the last six months. Community reaction has been largely negative, with major pain points including the need to manually check thousands of assets against the removal list. Industry analysis suggests this move aligns with Unity's separate ecosystem strategy for Tuanjie Engine, the China-localized Unity variant, signaling further regional fragmentation in the game engine market.
