## Liquid Swords: Why Choose UE5 for Developing "Samson"

<div class="article-meta">
  <span class="article-category">#UE5</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Open World</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Tech Stack</span>
</div>

Liquid Swords' latest open-world action game "Samson" is confirmed for release on April 8. Built on Unreal Engine 5, the game leverages three core technologies: Nanite, Lumen, and Virtual Shadow Maps. The studio notes that Nanite enables the art team to import cinema-quality detailed models without performance concerns; Lumen's real-time lighting system allows level designers to see final lighting results instantly, dramatically reducing lighting iteration cycles; while Virtual Shadow Maps solves the shadow quality vs. performance trade-off in large open-world scenes.

### Key Technology: Nanite Gives Artists More Freedom

Nanite's virtualized geometry technology allows developers to use high-poly models (millions to billions of polygons) directly without traditional LOD workflows. For technical designers, this means being able to validate with near-final quality assets during the prototyping phase, reducing iteration costs from gray-box to final results. Additionally, Nanite automatically handles LOD switching and culling, allowing teams to focus on gameplay design.

### Lumen Real-Time Lighting Revolutionizes Workflow

Traditional lighting baking workflows require hours or even days of waiting time, making any adjustments costly. Lumen's fully dynamic lighting allows designers to adjust light positions and parameters in real-time, seeing results instantly. This not only improves iteration efficiency but also makes "gameplay-driven lighting design" possible—for example, dynamically adjusting environmental atmosphere based on game pacing without worrying about baking limitations.

## The Witcher 4 to Be Showcased at UE Fest 2026, Promises Better Performance

CDPR has confirmed that "The Witcher 4" will be built on the latest version of Unreal Engine 5 and will be officially showcased at Unreal Engine Fest in June 2026. Notably, the development team specifically emphasized that the game will "run way better than a lot of other UE5 games"—suggesting they may have mastered more mature Nanite and Lumen optimization solutions, addressing the performance bottlenecks that many UE5 games currently face.

### Migration Considerations: From REDengine to UE5

CDPR's decision to abandon their proprietary engine in favor of UE5 reflects an industry trend: when engine technology complexity reaches a certain point, the cost of maintaining a proprietary engine may exceed its benefits. For technical designers, this means being able to focus more on gameplay innovation rather than底层技术维护. Meanwhile, UE5's mature toolchain (such as MetaHuman, Motion Warping) can also accelerate prototype validation workflows.

### Key Directions for UE5 Performance Optimization

Common performance issues in current UE5 games include: GPU overhead of Lumen in complex scenes, triangle overflow in Nanite at specific viewing angles, and stuttering in World Partition streaming. CDPR's optimization approach may involve: tiered Lumen quality settings, fine-grained Nanite culling strategies, and smarter texture streaming mechanisms. These insights are worth considering for technical designers at the early stages of projects.

## FF7 Remake Part 3: Why Not Switch to UE5?

<div class="article-meta">
  <span class="article-category">#Engine Migration</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Project Decisions</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Tech Debt</span>
</div>

"Final Fantasy VII Remake" Part 3 will continue development using Unreal Engine 4. Director Naoki Hamaguchi explains that while UE5's Lumen and Nanite are tempting, migrating to a new engine would "risk our existing progress." This decision reveals the real dilemma technical designers face mid-project: the allure of new technology vs. the actual cost of migration.

### The Real Cost of Engine Migration

Hamaguchi notes that the team has invested heavily in customizations for UE4, including unique rendering pipelines, animation systems, and toolchains. Migrating to UE5 would mean adapting or rewriting all this work. For projects already in mid-to-late development, such migration often leads to delays of 6-12 months or more. Technical designers need to assess at the project planning stage: is it worth taking this risk for new technology?

### When to Stick with Existing Solutions

The FF7 team's decision provides a reference framework: when a project has established stable workflows, significant investments in customized tools, and the existing engine can meet core requirements, sticking with the current solution may be the more pragmatic choice. Technical designers need to find balance between "chasing new technology" and "ensuring delivery."

## GitHub Agentic Workflows: AI-Powered Codebase Automation

<div class="article-meta">
  <span class="article-category">#AI Tools</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Automation</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Workflow</span>
</div>

GitHub has released the Agentic Workflows feature, leveraging large language models to achieve codebase automation. Developers only need to describe automation goals and expected results in Markdown, and AI agents can execute these instructions through GitHub Actions. This provides technical designers with a new automation approach: defining toolchain workflows in natural language.

### Automation Opportunities in Game Development

Game projects typically involve large amounts of repetitive work: asset import checks, level naming convention validation, performance test report generation, etc. Traditional approaches require writing complex scripts or editor plugins, with high maintenance costs. The Agentic Workflows model—describing requirements in natural language and letting AI generate implementations—may lower the barrier to such automation.

### Practical Scenarios for Technical Designers

Technical designers can explore the following automation scenarios:
- **Level Specification Checks**: Automatically verify naming conventions, component configurations, collision settings
- **Performance Regression Detection**: Automatically run performance tests and compare with historical data
- **Documentation Sync**: Automatically update relevant technical documentation when code changes
- **Build Validation**: Automated smoke testing to ensure critical paths are playable

## The Rising Wave of UGC Creators: From Genshin Impact's Realm of Wonders

<div class="article-meta">
  <span class="article-category">#UGC</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Editor</span>
  <span class="article-category">#Creator Economy</span>

In October 2025, Genshin Impact's "Realm of Wonders" launched, allowing players to create and share custom levels through an editor. This marks a new stage for UGC (User Generated Content): not just simple map editing, but complete gameplay creation tools. Full-time UGC creators are beginning to emerge, earning stable income through content creation.

### UGC Design from a Technical Designer's Perspective

From Minecraft to Genshin Impact's Realm of Wonders, successful UGC platforms share some common characteristics:
- **Tiered Tools**: Providing both simple drag-and-drop templates and deep scripting capabilities
- **Social Discovery**: Content needs to be seen; recommendation algorithms and social sharing mechanisms are crucial
- **Economic Loop**: Creators need real returns to sustain long-term investment

### How to Design Game Editors

When planning editors, technical designers need to consider:
- **Learning Curve**: Can new users produce their first playable content within 5 minutes?
- **Expression Ceiling**: Can power users achieve complexity接近 official content?
- **Error Handling**: When users make mistakes, how does the system gracefully guide rather than crash?
